# Revision And Review

Use this file for polishing, continuing, revising, reviewer response work, and pre-submission checks.

## First Diagnosis

Before editing, extract:

- Current research question.
- Claimed contribution.
- Closest literature or missing citation slots.
- Model family and equilibrium concept.
- Players, strategies, timing, information, payoffs.
- Main propositions/results.
- Evidence, simulation, or case support.
- Weakest section.
- Mathematical TODOs.
- Reference TODOs.

If the paper lacks a clear contribution, fix that before polishing sentences.

## Revision Passes

1. **Contribution and logic pass**
   - Align introduction gap, model assumptions, propositions, and conclusion.
   - Remove claims that the model does not prove.
   - Make the marginal contribution explicit relative to the closest literature.
2. **Model and proof pass**
   - Verify notation, domains, FOCs, SOCs, equilibrium concept, parameter restrictions, and comparative statics.
   - Move long algebra to appendix.
   - Add intuition paragraphs after technical results.
3. **Structure and readability pass**
   - Reorder sections so each result answers the research question.
   - Add signposting at section openings and transitions.
   - Ensure every figure/table has a purpose.
4. **Language and format pass**
   - Remove vague adjectives and repeated wording.
   - Standardize terms, notation, citations, formula numbering, figure/table labels.

## Self-Review Checklist

Ask these before calling a draft ready:

1. Can the paper be explained in 30 seconds: question, model, result, importance?
2. Does the introduction gap match the model's actual contribution?
3. Is the marginal contribution explicit relative to the closest work?
4. Does the result pass the "So what?" test?
5. Is every core assumption justified by intuition or literature?
6. Which conclusion depends on which assumption?
7. Are all parameters defined once and used consistently?
8. Does the model setup follow intuition, formalization, definition?
9. Have all proofs or derivations been independently checked?
10. Does every proposition have proof and intuition?
11. Is the intuition a mechanism explanation, not a formula paraphrase?
12. Are any "external" variables actually endogenous?
13. Can every figure/table stand alone with title, labels, legends, and notes?
14. Does the case mapping truly match the model?
15. Are comparative statics rich enough to support the implications?
16. Is at least one robustness or extension included when the main assumption is fragile?
17. Do abstract and conclusion correspond to the actual paper?
18. Do all in-text citations appear in references and all references appear in text?
19. Is language concise and academic?
20. Does formatting match the target outlet or assignment?

## Simulated Reviewer Test

Write a mock review with:

- One-paragraph neutral summary.
- 1-3 major concerns.
- Minor concerns.
- Recommendation: reject, major revision, minor revision, or accept.

Major concern categories:

- **Contribution unclear**: the paper seems too close to existing literature or lacks a new mechanism.
- **Model unconvincing**: a key assumption feels ad hoc, unrealistic, or inconsistent with the motivation.
- **Analysis incomplete**: missing equilibrium cases, weak comparative statics, no intuition, unsupported policy claims.

Use the mock review as a revision action list.

## Handling Mathematical Difficulty

If the model cannot be solved cleanly:

1. Diagnose the cause:
   - Algebra too complex but theoretically solvable.
   - Objective not concave.
   - Multiple local optima.
   - Pure-strategy equilibrium may not exist.
   - Timing/information assumptions are inconsistent.
2. Choose a path:
   - **Strategic simplification**: simplify functions, reduce players, discretize strategies, reintroduce a standard assumption.
   - **Numerical analysis**: keep complexity, solve under transparent parameterization, and visualize mechanisms.
   - **Nonexistence result**: prove nonexistence and interpret instability as the contribution.
3. Explain the choice honestly in the paper.

## Polishing Existing Text

For each paragraph, check:

- Topic sentence states the point.
- Evidence/model result supports the point.
- The mechanism is explicit.
- No unsupported overclaim.
- No redundant phrase.
- Terms and symbols match the rest of the paper.

For Chinese academic polishing:

- Replace casual or rhetorical wording with precise analytical language.
- Use compact transition phrases: "进一步地", "与基准模型相比", "该结果表明", "其经济学直觉在于".
- Avoid inflated claims such as "具有极强价值" unless supported.

For English academic polishing:

- Prefer direct economics prose: "The result shows that...", "This mechanism operates through...", "Compared with the benchmark...".
- Avoid vague intensifiers and long noun stacks.

## R&R Response Workflow

1. Build a comment matrix:
   - ID.
   - Reviewer.
   - Original comment.
   - Category.
   - Planned action.
   - Manuscript location.
   - Status.
2. Group repeated concerns across reviewers.
3. Revise the manuscript first, then write the response letter.
4. Response letter structure:
   - Thank editor and reviewers.
   - Summarize major revisions.
   - Quote each comment.
   - Acknowledge the concern.
   - State exact changes with section/page/paragraph/line if available.
5. If disagreeing:
   - Acknowledge the point.
   - Explain why the original approach is appropriate for the paper's scope.
   - Add a clarification, limitation, or robustness check when possible.

## Revision Memo Template

After revising, output:

- Major structural changes.
- Model/proof changes.
- New or revised propositions.
- Literature/citation changes.
- Language/style changes.
- Remaining risks or TODOs.
- Suggested next upgrade.
